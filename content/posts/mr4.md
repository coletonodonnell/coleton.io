Few artists of the 21st Century are as talented as Chicago's own Ye (formally known as Kanye West.) Ye has produced some of the greatest albums of the 2010's and will go down as one of (if not the greatest) hip hop artist to ever walk the face of the Earth. At least, he'd have you believe that. Kid Cudi, although less renowned, is not to be ignored in comparison to Ye, and he has many classics under his belt. Cudi and Ye are wonderful solo artists, but how about collaborative? Well, these two forces come together in KIDS SEE GHOSTS. As a duo under the name KIDS SEE GHOSTS, Ye and Cudi release a single self-titled album. As a project, this is one of the most creative and groundbreaking hip hop albums I have heard, as well as one of the most powerful, as in the case of "Cudi Montage." 

The track runs at around three minutes and change, and features a eerie and grimy guitar sample laced throughout the beat by none other than Kurt Cobain. This guitar sets the stage for Cudi's first verse, where he states that God has given him a mission to save the world through his lyrics, but that the nature of this assignment is a lot for Cudi, and he begins to plea with God to "shine [his] love on [him]."  After this, the Chorus sets, continuing the theme of desire for God's help. Next comes Ye's verse, which includes some incredibly clever world play describing the perpetual cycle of violence within Chicago:

> Everybody want world peace   
'Til your niece get shot in the dome-piece  
Then you go and buy your own piece  
Hopin' it'll help you find your own peace  

These lyrics are delivered in a firm resolve not unique to this song, but under the expression of these lyrics, comes off stronger than usual. Each bar that Ye spits flushes out the theme of crime and the prison system. It is a wonderful passage and is worth studying.
